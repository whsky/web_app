How It Works

  • Player-Level Data

    Rather than just using a team's season average, I collected player-level data. Data from boxscores like minutes played, assists, points scored, and turn overs were put into a dataframe, for every game. Using this highly granular data gives modeling more chances to find patterns in player performance to predict the point spread. With some serious pandas kung-fu, this data can be maniputed to lend itself perfectly as an input to a Neural Net.

  • Machine Learning

    Using the player data, we feed in each season's games to make predictions using a Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Net (MLP-NN). In essence, MLP-NNs are fantastic at recognizing patterns. They were invented to behave similarly to the way neurons in our brains behave. As Siegrid LÃ¶wel said: "Cells that fire together, wire together." Patterns of repeated player performance in the input which correlate to a large margin of victory then get weighted heavily in our model.

  • A Rank Agnostic Approach

    Current modeling often uses a team's rank or tournament seed into consideration when predicting a game's outcome. At first glance this appears logical, a better ranked team should beat a worse ranked team, right? Well, the problem with that approach is that tournament seeding is mostly determined by a panel chosen by the NCAA. Using just player performance as an input, we can avoid any outside influence on expected performance.

How Well Does It Work

Every year hundreds of Division I teams play thousands of games, all of them trying to make it to NCAA's March Madness Tournament.

  • 351 Teams
  • 5,612 Games
  • 6,840 Players
  • 5,751,270 Datapoints
  • 1 Champion

We can use these millions of datapoints to help us predict tournament game outcomes. In last year's tournament, my model correctly called 62% of victors of games played with an average error in point-spreads of 11.5 points per game. I have also modified this model to conform with entry into Kaggle's March Machine Learning Mania 2017, so keep an eye on the Leaderboard to see how well it stacks up!
